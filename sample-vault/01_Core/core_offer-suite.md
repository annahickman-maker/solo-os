## The ladder

The main ladder is deliberately simple. Three rungs.

1. **Discover Anna on YouTube** (@theannahickman) - long-form videos as the trust-building engine
2. **Join Solopreneur Systems** ($47/mo) - the on-ramp into the full operating system + community + weekly Q&A calls. Every YouTube CTA points here.
3. **OS Builds** ($10K+, 1/month, inbound only) - for high-ticket clients who'd rather have it built than build it themselves. SS membership folded in for life.

That's the whole funnel. No detour layer. No "first grab a freebie then upgrade" path.

### What about the freebies + Gumroad products?

They exist. They still generate passive revenue (~$1K/month). They're not being killed. But:

- They're **parked, not promoted.** Anna isn't sending YouTube traffic to them anymore.
- They were built when web design services were the focus. The system has moved past that.
- The plan: the most useful Gumroad templates (Web Design Workflow, Freelance Dashboard, Client Portal) get folded into Solopreneur Systems as **AI-powered modules** inside the Solopreneur OS. The Web Design module + Client OS module both plug into the OS itself.
- Anything that doesn't make sense as an SS module stays on Gumroad as passive long-tail revenue.

So the offer suite has two layers:
- **Active funnel (where attention goes):** YouTube → SS → OS Builds
- **Passive long-tail (still earning, not being grown):** Gumroad products + free lead magnets

## Main Offer (business focus)

**Offer:** Solopreneur Systems
**Link:** https://www.skool.com/mastermind-5724/about
**Price:** $47/month (rising by $10 for every 10 new members - early signups get the lowest rate locked in)

### Why it's a membership, not a one-time course

The Solopreneur OS is a system built around you as a human and your business. That part doesn't change. But the tools that power it - AI, the skills layer, the specific workflows that actually work right now - are constantly evolving.

A one-time course would be stale in six months. A membership is anchored to the reality: AI and the systems you use are ever-changing, and members keep up with the latest as Anna ships it.

Every week a new feature gets built inside SS - a new skill, a workflow update, a tool integration. Members get it the day it ships. The weekly Q&A calls reinforce the live, evolving nature of it.

So the subscription isn't paying for access to information - it's paying for the operating system to keep current with where the work actually is.

### Who it's NOT for

- **Beginners with no skill or business yet.** SS is for creatives who already have a skill, an existing service business (even small), and some traction. Someone starting completely from zero would need to build the foundational skill first before this system would compound.
- **People who want done-for-you, not done-with-you.** If someone wants Anna to build their OS for them, that's OS Builds at $10K+ - not SS. SS is for builders.
- **People looking for AI/Claude tutorials or "the latest tool" content.** SS is about the system around the human - not about the AI tooling itself. Someone who's there for AI-tutorial-of-the-week energy will be disappointed.
- **Hustle-and-grind seekers.** SS is built around 2-hour days, lean operation, sustainable cadence. People who want intensity-as-identity won't get what they're looking for.
- **People who want a course library and won't show up.** The weekly Q&A calls are the differentiator. Members who never come to calls only get half the value.

### Implementation gap

Anna's free content (YouTube videos) shows what the systems look like and walks through what she's building each week. Anyone can watch and try to replicate it themselves.

What SS gives that free content can't:

- **The actual vault template.** Members install the same operating system Anna uses - voice onboarding, skills library, brain layer. Not a tutorial; the working system.
- **Weekly Q&A calls.** Live group coaching where members get direct feedback on their business, content, and offers. This is the human element that doesn't exist in a video.
- **The community.** Other creator entrepreneurs building alongside each other. Accountability, feedback, shared wins, hot seats.
- **The build-and-document cadence.** Every week a new feature ships inside SS. Members get it the day Anna builds it - the video about it lands publicly later.
- **Specific implementation help.** Free content shows the principle. SS gives the actual templates, prompts, and skill files, plus help applying them to a member's specific business.

### Connection to your work

The natural bridge is the **weekly flywheel** itself. Every YouTube video documents a feature Anna just built inside SS. So the CTA writes itself:

> *"If you want the [thing I just showed you], it's inside Solopreneur Systems - link in description."*

The work creates the desire (someone watches Anna build a thing and wants it for themselves), and SS is exactly what fills the gap (the thing, the template, the community, the help).

Other bridges that work:
- A video about positioning → "If you want my full positioning workflow, it's inside SS."
- A video showing a client process → "I built this with [client] using the OS - if you want to use the same OS, it's inside SS."
- A video about content workflow → "The skill that wrote this video is inside the SS skills library."

The offer is always framed as the shortcut, never the necessity.

### Won't do (anti-patterns)

These are the things Anna deliberately doesn't do when talking about SS:

- **No hard sell.** Soft invite only. The offer is always positioned as optional, after the viewer has already received full value from the content.
- **Pre-dismiss the need.** Always include some version of: "You don't need me. You can absolutely do this without me. But if you do want the systems I use..."
- **No fake urgency.** No countdown timers, no "this offer closes in 24 hours" pressure. The price-rising-as-members-join mechanic is the only urgency - and that's real, not manufactured.
- **No guru language.** No "transform your business," no "unlock your potential," no "exclusive insider access."
- **No "passive income" promises.** Anna actively corrects this language: "Passive income doesn't really exist." Use "scalable income" or "income that doesn't require more hours" instead.
- **No hustle culture endorsement.** Don't promise quick or easy. "If it were easy, everyone would be doing it."
- **No mid-video paid offer pitches.** Mid-video CTAs can point to another video or a free resource - but never a hard sell on the paid offer mid-content.
- **No "Lambo and laptop on the beach" energy.** Cozy lifestyle, two-hour days, sustainable income - that's the picture. Not lifestyle porn.
