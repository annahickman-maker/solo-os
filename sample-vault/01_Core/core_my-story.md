# Core My Story

**Story structure for content use:**

---

## Chapter 1 - Origin: 2020

My journey starts in 2020. I had just launched my web design business and had no idea how I was going to find clients.

But I had zero interest in hunting down clients and begging them to pay me.

So I started posting random tips on TikTok, crossing my fingers that someone would want to work with me.

And it worked. I would post a few videos and see leads coming into my inbox.

This shit was fun. Until it wasn't.

---

## Chapter 2 - What wasn't working

I quickly realized that creating content is exhausting.

When I was posting, things were going great and I had a steady stream of clients. But I would very quickly burn out, tired of churning out endless content that lacked any real strategy.

And on top of that, my follower count was growing but my income stayed the same. I was capped at $10K/month and I had no interest or energy to take on more clients.

So for the next 2 years I tried everything else I could think of to grow my income.

Testing day rates. Building a course. Flirting with digital products. I even spent months building out a web design template, with all the tutorials ready, only to post about it once and then never talk about it again.

By March 2023, I was completely burnt out.

---

## Chapter 3 - The turning point

So I quit.

I stopped taking new clients. Scrapped my business. Started from scratch.

But this time, instead of bending over backwards trying to please the algorithms, I focused on simply serving my audience. I learned what they needed. I tested and launched simple products that solved real problems.

---

## Chapter 4 - What I did

For 6 months I made zero money.

By September 2023, I found myself completely broke. On the phone to my mum in tears, ready to give up.

Then the next week, I had my first successful launch and made $1,500 in the first 24 hours.

---

## Chapter 5 - The results

From there it scaled fast. By February 2024 I was hitting consistent 5-figure months:

- February 2024: $41,202.87
- March 2024: $52,503.56
- April 2024: $29,649.39

First year on YouTube: $320,080. To date: $349,316+ in digital product sales. The original videos kept selling for years - $20K/month on autopilot from videos I posted once.

No team. No complex funnels. No churning out endless content.

Just the right offer. And a simple but effective system that turns content into customers. Even when I'm offline.

---

## Chapter 6 - What this taught me

The best part: I now have the skills, the systems, and the leverage to sell whatever I put my name to.

I can build a business that lets me work when I want, with who I want, on whatever the fuck I want.

The bigger lessons that came out of it:

- I didn't need a bigger audience. I had enough. The work was converting what I already had, not chasing more reach.
- I didn't need more consistency. I needed a focused sprint and a system that fit my brain.
- I didn't need to be the best at anything. I needed to be best at packaging and communicating what I already knew.
- The "broken brain" wasn't broken. It was just trying to run someone else's playbook.
- Long-form beats short-form when you're trying to sell.
- YouTube isn't a views platform - it's a sales platform. Trust converts.
- Information is a commodity. Insight is rare.
- The most valuable thing I can do is give the knowledge away free and sell the implementation.

---

## Chapter 7 - Current chapter

Now I'm building the Solopreneur OS - the actual operating system I use to run my one-person business. I'm teaching creative freelancers how to build the same kind of business: premium clients at the top, a content system that doubles as a sales engine, digital products and community filling in the gap.

Solopreneur Systems is where I share the playbook at $47/month. OS Builds is where I build it custom for high-ticket clients at $10K+, capped at one a month.

The work is the proof. Every week I add a new feature to the Solopreneur OS, document the build, and turn it into a YouTube video. That's the flywheel.
