# Core Audience

## Name

**Adriana** is the canonical persona. Use this name in every downstream content, copy, and strategy decision.

A full deep-dive avatar exists at `05_Assets/Avatars/avatar-adriana.md` - built from 6 real coaching call transcripts. There's also `05_Assets/Avatars/avatar-past-anna.md` for the past-Anna persona. Read those files when pressure-testing video ideas, content angles, or titles.

This file is the master summary. The avatar file is the source.

## Day-to-Day Reality

Adriana is an experienced creative freelancer with a real business and an audience she has worked hard to build. From the outside she looks like she's doing well. From the inside it's a rollercoaster.

A normal Tuesday:

- She spends a lot of time scrolling other creators' content, taking notes. "Oh, this person - I see what they're doing. How can I apply it to my business?" Then onto the next person. Then the next.
- She gets pulled in many directions by different ideas - new product ideas, new launch angles, new sales page approaches.
- She says yes to clients that aren't aligned because she needs the money.
- She fills her day with busy work that isn't actually productive - strategizing different products she could launch, planning, building offer concepts.
- She avoids the thing that would actually move her forward: building the content, honing the sales page, shipping.
- She builds a lot. She doesn't publish.

The pattern underneath all of it: she's exhausted with content but knows she needs it. She's on a treadmill - post to get clients, get pulled into client work, drop content, feel like she's starting from zero, repeat.

## Limiting Mindsets

The specific thought patterns and behaviours keeping her stuck:

1. **"If I had a bigger audience, this would work."** She believes the missing piece is reach. Reality: she has enough audience to start.
2. **"I have to stay open to all client types because I need the money."** She doesn't believe she's allowed to be selective yet.
3. **"Saying no and giving myself more time feels impossible."** Cutting back feels reckless when income is uneven.
4. **"I have to launch the perfect thing. Everything has to be built out before I can put anything out there."** Perfectionism dressed up as preparation.
5. **"Who the hell is going to listen to me? Why does anybody need another internet guru?"** She conflates being a creator with being a guru and recoils from both.

**New mindset patterns from the Q&A and hot-seat transcripts:**

6. **"Everything has to be original, otherwise I'm ripping someone off."** AT and Jasmine both wrestled with this around Storybrand. They feel guilty for using any framework they learned somewhere else. *"How do I do that without just outright ripping off Donald Miller?"* (AT, Q&A 22 Oct). *"I'm asking myself the exact same question, especially this week."* (Jasmine, same call). The block is moral as much as creative.

7. **"I have to learn everything before I can charge for it."** *"In web design there's a lot of us who will sell the service before we even know how to deliver it. Because they know I go to this place that will teach me how to do it. I can buy this course."* (AT, Q&A 22 Oct). The reverse - learn first, then sell - is the dominant mindset that's blocking action.

8. **"If I get more time to think, the work will be better."** *"I'm gonna just be more creative... If I get time to sleep on it."* (Jasmine, 7 Jan). Used as a justification for endless flexibility with clients, which then becomes scope creep.

9. **"I can't have boundaries because I need the money / I'm new."** *"It might just be being new, and I can't really be picky right now."* (Jasmine, 7 Jan). The story she tells to justify staying unboundaried.

10. **"If I niche too hard, I'll get stuck."** *"I feel like the second that I start making content, I'm just going to get hyper-siloed into like this one thing."* (Hot Seat 3 member). The fear that picking a niche permanently closes off other paths.

11. **"I have to wait for [permission / health insurance / a backup income / the perfect setup] before I can really commit."** AT: *"One of the main things that I have to figure out before I can fully leave is health insurance."* The legitimate-sounding waiting condition that keeps her hedging.

12. **"Anything I'm not paying full attention to is going to break."** *"Three backups: two local, one in the cloud. The vault that lives only on your desktop is the vault you lose."* The deeper pattern: she doesn't trust herself or her systems to hold, so she over-manages everything.

13. **"My first video has to be the one that pops off."** *"My first video, like, what if I like, it has to be perfect, you know, has to call out exactly to like the exact niche."* (Hot Seat 3 member). The pressure she puts on any single piece of content.

14. **"I should be further along by now."** Across almost every call. *"It needs to be done already. Let's go. I'm so far behind on my personal deadline."* (Rob, 2026-05-20). The constant background drumbeat.

The reframe (content seed): *"Content isn't about being an internet guru. It's about building the reputation you want to be known for - around the thing you're actually good at."*

## Content They Consume

**Types:**
- Income and freedom stories - anyone talking about making money in a way that buys back time
- Digital products revenue reveals - especially passive-style income
- Personal brand how-to - specifically about doing it seamlessly and consistently
- Business model breakdowns - how other one-person businesses are actually structured
- Behind-the-scenes of creators living the lifestyle she wants at the income she wants. She binges everything they publish.

**Sources:**
- Tom Noske
- Matt Gray
- Dan Koe
- (and the wider one-person-business / solopreneur creator orbit)

**What lands hardest right now:**
Raw, relatable content where someone is sharing their own real experience - the kind where Adriana can see herself in it and see the path forward. *Paired with* very practical, concrete advice. Mindset and how-to in the same piece. Not pure information. Not pure motivation. The combination is what makes it land.

**What she would NOT click (from avatar file):**
- "How to go viral" - she does not want that
- "Scale your coaching business" - too corporate, too fast
- Generic freelance advice not specific to her world
- Anything that assumes she wants to hustle more
