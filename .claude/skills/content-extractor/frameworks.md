# Carousel + Caption Frameworks

Structural reference for `content-extractor`. Distilled from external IP (Maha Copy Co. - 265 Titles, 16 Carousel Formulas, 100 Caption Templates) into bone-only skeletons translated to the creator's voice. The structures are the value. The phrasings are NOT carried over.

**Critical rule:** when this file is consulted, the skill uses the *structure* but writes copy from `01_Core/core_voice-style.md` and `03_Projects/instagram/instagram-context.md`. Never use any phrasing from the Maha source materials directly.

## Part 1 - Content categories (atom classifier)

23 categories. When extracting atoms, tag each with the best-fit category. The category drives which carousel formula to use.

| # | Category | Use when atom is... |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Problem Awareness | Pointing out a problem people don't realise they have, or correcting a widely-held belief |
| 2 | Actionable Tips | Step-by-step or list-style how-to for one specific outcome |
| 3 | Value Shares | A truth, framework, or perspective shift on a topic |
| 4 | Plug-and-Play | A drop-in resource - templates, scripts, prompts, swipes |
| 5 | Expert Showcase | How the creator specifically approaches a task others get wrong |
| 6 | Business Insider | A counterintuitive choice in how the creator runs her business |
| 7 | Personal Introductions | Who the creator is / who this account is for |
| 8 | Story Shares | A specific narrative with a turning point and lesson |
| 9 | Behind the Scenes | What building something actually looks like in practice |
| 10 | Engagement Invitations | Quiz / pick-one / which-are-you formats (use sparingly - low quality signal) |
| 11 | Reflections & Recaps | What changed and what the creator learned, after the fact |
| 12 | Case Studies | A specific client or self transformation, with numbers |
| 13 | Struggle Shares | Naming a hard moment without solving it - permission, not prescription |
| 14 | Turning Points | The moment a belief shifted, with what was let go |
| 15 | Before & Afters | Side-by-side state change with the bridge action between |
| 16 | Visualizations & Projections | "If you start today, here's what changes by month X" |
| 17 | Portfolio Shares | Final reveals, project details, the craft itself |
| 18 | Offer Pitch | Direct - here's the thing, here's who it's for |
| 19 | The Only Option | Why the obvious alternative falls short for this specific reader |
| 20 | Self-Selection | "If you're X and want Y" - hand-raisers walk themselves in |
| 21 | Sliding Door Stories | The cost of waiting / not deciding |
| 22 | Excitement Builders | Launches, drops, freebies - announcement posts |

## Part 2 - The 16 carousel formulas (bone-only)

Each formula is a 10-slide skeleton. Slide numbers reference *role*, not rigid count - a body can compress to 4 slides or expand to 8.

The formulas group into four modes. The skill picks a formula internally based on the atom's category. **Mode labels (EDUCATE / RELATE / INSPIRE / SELL) stay implicit** - they do not surface in the user-facing content plan.

### EDUCATE mode

**E1 - Actionable Tips**
1. Hook (post title)
2. Optional context / what they may already know
3-8. One tip per slide (action + brief why)
9. Optional reframe / takeaway
10. CTA

**E2 - Expert Showcase**
1. Hook
2. The question worth asking
3. A reframe or example of what you mean
4. The insight (the aha)
5-8. Examples with the reasoning behind each choice
9. Optional takeaway
10. CTA

**E3 - Value Share**
1. Hook
2. A fact, observation, or surprising data point
3. The truth it reveals
4. The bigger principle
5-8. Examples / specific applications
9. Optional takeaway
10. CTA

**E4 - Plug-and-Play**
1. Hook
2. Optional categorisation of what's inside
3-9. The drop-in items
10. CTA

**E5 - Problem Awareness**
1. Hook
2. The real problem (named plainly)
3. The truth / perspective shift
4. Why the misdiagnosis happens (empathy, not blame)
5. Why it makes sense they got here
6. The reframe / what the goal actually requires
7. The alternative path
8. Concrete examples
9. Optional takeaway
10. CTA

### RELATE mode

**R1 - Reflections & Recaps**
1. Hook
2. Backstory (where the creator was)
3. The problem that surfaced
4. Why it mattered / what was at stake
5. The decision / change made
6-8. What happened next (concrete, not breathless)
9. The reflection - what the creator actually learned
10. CTA

**R2 - Story Shares**
1. Hook
2. A scene from the climax
3. Backstory of how the creator got there
4. The turning point
5. The realisation / mindset shift
6-9. The takeaways and changes that followed
10. CTA

**R3 - Behind the Scenes**
1. Hook
2. Context - what the creator is in the middle of
3. A problem that came up (keeps it real)
4. How the creator addressed it
5-7. Subsequent steps and adjustments
8. The final detail or last touch
9. The outcome
10. CTA

**R4 - Engagement Invitations**
1. Hook (question / pick-one)
2-9. Options
10. CTA

### INSPIRE mode

**I1 - Visualisations & Projections**
1. Hook
2. Optional starting point
3-7. The arc of progress over time
8. The long-term value
9. The "or you could keep doing what you're doing" pivot
10. CTA

**I2 - Case Study**
1. Hook
2. Context - who and what state
3. The reframing question that changed things
4. The steps taken in response
5. The piece the offer/method contributed
6-8. The results (with numbers if available)
9. The takeaway
10. CTA

**I3 - Before & After**
1. Hook
2. The "before" state, specifically
3. How the before built up over time
4. The turning point / decision to change
5-7. The new actions taken
8. The "after" state
9. The takeaway - permission, not prescription
10. CTA

**I4 - Portfolio Shares**
1. Hook
2. Project context (who, what, why)
3-9. Specific details of the work (one per slide, with the reasoning behind each choice)
10. CTA

### SELL mode

**S1 - The Only Option**
1. Hook
2. Context - the mistake being addressed
3. Why the alternative seems reasonable
4. Why it falls short for this specific reader
5. The specific consequences
6. What the alternative is missing
7. Why the creator's offer is built for this gap
8. The path they could keep on
9. The path they could switch to
10. CTA

**S2 - FAOs (Frequently Asked Objections)**
1. Hook
2. Context for the objection
3. The direct answer / truth
4. Why that answer makes sense
5-8. Real-world example or metaphor
9. The takeaway
10. CTA

**S3 - Self-Selection**
1. Hook (if you're X and want Y...)
2. Direct sell
3. Introduce the offer
4-9. Features and what each one does for the reader
10. CTA

## Part 3 - Title angle bank (5 per category, distilled)

the creator-voice angles, not Maha phrasings. Use during atom extraction to seed possible hook lines.

**1. Problem Awareness**
- The common advice you quietly disagree with, stated plainly
- The thing they think is the problem vs. what actually is
- An action they think helps but is actively making it worse
- A "stop doing X" with the real reason underneath
- The #1 reason this specific result keeps not happening

**2. Actionable Tips**
- "Struggling with X" → the steps
- How to do X without the usual Y (timeline, budget, or skill constraint)
- The X-step plan for a specific outcome
- What to do when you're already past the easy fixes
- The list of things that look like overkill but aren't

**3. Value Shares**
- The truth about X (no "secrets" - just the truth)
- X, finally explained without the jargon
- What no one says about X
- The thing that's more important than people think
- Why X happens (the mechanism, not just the symptom)

**4. Plug-and-Play**
- N items for [specific situation]
- The shortlist the creator actually uses
- The starter pack for [outcome]
- What to use when you don't want to think about it
- The smallest viable version of [system]

**5. Expert Showcase**
- How the creator does X (the actual process)
- the creator's method for [specific outcome]
- Why the creator always / never does X
- How the creator upgrades the basic version of X
- The decisions the creator makes that change the result

**6. Business Insider**
- Why the creator doesn't [common practice]
- What the creator stopped doing and what happened
- The thing the creator does that the playbook says not to
- the creator's [time period] in numbers
- The decision that quietly changed everything

**7. Personal Introductions**
- Who this is for, in one sentence
- What you'll see if you stay here
- the creator's one-line bio
- Why the creator built this account / this business
- The belief this account runs on

**8. Story Shares**
- The time X (specific, with a turn)
- The decision the creator nearly didn't make
- the creator's first try at X (and what it taught her)
- What happened when the creator stopped [necessary-seeming action]
- The mistake the creator made early on and what it cost

**9. Behind the Scenes**
- Building X in real time
- A day inside a one-person business
- How the creator actually does [recurring task]
- What this week looked like (concrete, not curated)
- The piece of the process nobody films

**10. Engagement Invitations**
- (Use sparingly - low signal-to-effort. Default away unless clearly the right format.)
- Pick one - which describes you
- What kind of [identity] are you
- Help the creator decide between X and Y

**11. Reflections & Recaps**
- Why the creator switched from X to Y
- The quarter / month / week in one paragraph
- What the creator's changing and why
- What the creator got wrong and the adjustment
- The decision that needed making and the reasoning

**12. Case Studies**
- How [client / the creator] went from X to Y in [time]
- The change that moved the number from N1 to N2
- Watch this go from [before] to [after]
- The boundary / decision / shift that made it work
- The numbers, then the story

**13. Struggle Shares**
- The hard part of [thing] nobody warns you about
- Naming it: this is what stuck feels like
- Why it makes sense that you're tired
- The week (or month) the creator nearly quit
- (Anti-pattern: never resolve the struggle in the carousel. Let it sit.)

**14. Turning Points**
- The belief the creator had to drop to move
- The "but what about" the creator kept hiding behind
- What if [alternative reframe] was actually the move
- The cost of the belief, made specific
- Permission to stop [the thing that's costing you]

**15. Before & Afters**
- The bridge action between these two states
- From [identity before] to [identity after] - and what changed
- The small daily action that compounded into this
- What changed when the creator did X every day for [time]
- The before nobody shares vs. the after

**16. Visualisations & Projections**
- If you start today, here's the path
- What changes by month / quarter / year
- The compounding curve, drawn out
- Two versions of you in 12 months
- What [time] of consistency actually buys you

**17. Portfolio Shares**
- Final reveal of [project]
- The detail nobody asked about but everyone needs
- The reasoning behind each choice
- The piece of the work that took longest
- How this turned out vs. the brief

**18. Offer Pitch**
- The offer, in one line
- Who this is for (and isn't)
- What's inside, no hype
- New: [thing]
- The thing the creator just shipped

**19. The Only Option**
- Why [common alternative] doesn't quite work for [specific reader]
- The thing alternatives miss
- Two paths - what each one actually delivers
- Why generic [solution] keeps failing this specific case
- What this specific reader needs that the obvious option doesn't have

**20. Self-Selection**
- If you're a [identity] who wants [outcome] but doesn't have [resource]
- For the [identity] in the [situation]
- Who this is for, specifically
- The hand-raise: if X resonates, this is for you
- When you want X but Y is in the way

**21. Sliding Door Stories**
- When you realise you should have [acted] N [time] ago
- The cost of waiting on [decision]
- Signs you've already passed the should-have point
- What it costs to keep DIY-ing this
- The moment the maths flips

**22. Excitement Builders**
- [Thing] is live
- Free: [thing]
- the creator just made [thing] - here's how to get it
- [Number] of [thing], if you want them
- The drop, plainly

**23. FAOs**
- The objection you don't want to say out loud
- "What if I [reason it won't work for me]"
- Why the creator built this even though [common objection]
- The thing people worry about that doesn't actually matter
- You don't need [common expectation] to [outcome]

## Part 4 - Caption skeleton (7-part)

Every IG caption follows the same 7-part bone structure. Each part is one short paragraph or a few lines. Total caption length: short (3-6 lines) for low-friction posts, long (10-20 lines) for teaching posts. Never longer than the carousel deserves.

1. **Hook line** - first sentence. Stops the thumb. Does not preview the carousel slide-1 verbatim - it complements it. Often a specific observation, a quiet contradiction, or a flat declarative statement.
2. **Relatability / acknowledgement** - one sentence that names the reader's actual state. Not "raise your hand if" - just the observation. ("If you've been doing X and it isn't working, you're probably tired of hearing Y.")
3. **Context** - the situation in 1-3 sentences. What's true that makes the rest of this matter.
4. **Pivot / insight** - the turn. The "but here's what's actually going on" without the "BUT HERE'S WHAT'S ACTUALLY GOING ON" framing. State it plainly.
5. **Body** - the substance. Can be: bullets, a short list, a story beat, a few examples. This is where the carousel's argument lives in caption form.
6. **Synthesis** - one line that lands the point. Not a takeaway slogan. A sentence that lets the reader leave with one clear thing.
7. **CTA** - the main CTA from `instagram-context.md`, written in the creator's voice. Often a soft invite, not an instruction.

Hashtags sit below the caption in their own block, drawn from the pool in `instagram-context.md`. Never inline.

## Part 5 - the creator voice anti-patterns (the kill list)

Do not produce any of these in slide copy, hook lines, or captions. These are the tells that content was written from a generic template.

**Filler enthusiasm:**
- "Listen up, y'all" / "Psst..." / "Heads up"
- "Can we all agree on something?" / "Raise your hand if..."
- "Hear me out" / "Stick with me here" / "Allow me to elaborate"
- "Lemme break it down" / "*deep breath*"
- "Brace yourself" / "Believe it or not" / "Hold on to your hats"
- "And honestly..." / "Like, seriously" / "TBH"

**Hype payoffs:**
- "Hallelujah" / "Cue the music" / "Can you believe it?"
- "And on top of all that..." / "And the best part?"
- "MIND-BLOWING" / "GAME-CHANGING" / "LIFE-CHANGING"
- "AND YET..." / "BUT THEN..." (especially in all caps)

**Engagement bait:**
- "Comment YES below" / "Tag a friend who needs this"
- "Save this post for later" (only if `instagram-context.md` explicitly allows; default no)
- "Double-tap if..."
- "Drop a [emoji] in the comments"

**Bro-marketing / guru:**
- "Steal my..." (use "here's mine" or "you can have this" instead)
- "Secrets the gurus don't want you to know"
- "The X-figure / 6-figure / 7-figure [anything]" as bait
- "I went from broke to..." framing

**Visual yelling:**
- ALL CAPS sentences (one word for emphasis is fine, sentences are not)
- Multiple exclamation marks. One is rare, two is never.
- Em dashes anywhere (vault-wide rule)
- Asterisks-around-words for *italic emphasis* unless quoting

**Vague openers (slide-1):**
- "Let's talk about..." / "Here's the thing..." / "So..."
- "PSA:" / "Real talk:"
- "Unpopular opinion:" (the opinion should make this self-evident)

## Part 6 - Hook patterns for slide 1

The patterns the creator's hook slide can draw from. Pattern, not phrasing - the words come from the atom and `core_voice-style.md`.

- **Specific number + plain noun.** "Three slides that decide a sale." Not "5 SHOCKING tips."
- **Contrarian flip without the label.** State the opposing view as fact. Don't announce it's contrarian.
- **If/then conditional.** "If your offer page is busier than your home page, that's the problem."
- **The plain question.** "Who is your offer actually for?"
- **The misdiagnosis call.** "[Common thing they think is the problem] is not the problem."
- **The named cost.** "Every week without a system costs you one client conversation."
- **The before/after compression.** "From three offers to one. Revenue up."
- **The list opener.** "Four things that ship before a launch." (Followed by the four on the next slides.)
- **The flat declarative.** No ornament. "Niches don't sell. Specificity does."
- **The receipt.** A number, a quote, a screenshot caption, used as the hook.

Avoid: rhetorical questions that mask weak content, double meanings, puns, anything that needs the body slides to make sense.
