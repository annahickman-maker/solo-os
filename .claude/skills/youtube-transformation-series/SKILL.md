# YouTube Transformation Series

Plans a 5-video series that clearly communicates the user's method for helping people get the desirable result the channel exists to deliver.

**The structure:**
Each of the 5 videos maps 1:1 to one step in the user's core IP method. Each video delivers real value on its step - it's not a hook video or a results video, it's a teaching video that fits into the larger arc.

Video 1 has a special job: it sets up the overall series transformation (paints the picture of the desirable result) AND teaches the first step. So it does double duty - hooks people into the whole journey while delivering value on step 1. But the same is true for every video: each one stands alone with its own value AND builds toward the desirable result.

**Why split into 5 videos:**
Breaking the method into 5 standalone videos:
- Makes the series more manageable for the user to create
- Makes each video more watchable for the audience
- Creates 5 entry points instead of 1 long video
- Builds a body of work that establishes authority on this transformation

Optionally, after the 5 are published, the user can combine them into one long tutorial video and publish that too as a single comprehensive guide.

**Goal of the series:**
Give everything away for free. Sell the implementation.

The series teaches the full method end-to-end. The implementation helper, paid product, or service is positioned where it's most naturally relevant - like a regular mid-video CTA. It's NOT formulaic ("video 5 is the sales pitch"). It's contextual: the CTA lands in the videos where the user is naturally referencing the thing the offer delivers (e.g., showing the templates → "you can grab these templates below").

**Judgment needed:**
What each video looks like depends on:
- What kind of content the user makes (tutorial-heavy vs storytelling-heavy)
- Delivery style (talking head vs demo-heavy vs scripted)
- The user's offer (what they're funnelling toward)
- The user's method (how they help people get the result)

The skill uses judgment to shape the series to fit the user's specific channel - not a template applied blindly.

## Step 2 - Define the core result for each video

Work through each video in order. For each one, ask:

> "What is the result someone walks away with after watching video [number] - [step name]?
>
> Not the topic. The result.
>
> Nobody wakes up saying 'I want to [topic name]'. They wake up saying things like 'I want to get paid more for my time' or 'I'm sick of low-budget clients'. What is someone actually hoping to get from this video?"

Push for the audience's own language - not the polished/marketing version.

If they frame it around the topic, push:
> "Why does someone care about [topic]? What do they actually want for themselves at the end of it?"

Confirm each result before moving to the next.

STOP - once all 5 results are locked, reflect them back.

---

## Step 3 - Build the outline for each video

For each video, ask:

> "What are the 3-5 things you're going to cover in this video?
>
> The main moves - the steps someone takes or the ideas they need to understand to get to that result."

Push back on anything vague, generic, or not connected to their specific POV from `core_ip.md`.

Confirm each outline. Once all 5 are done, reflect the full series map:

```
Video 1 - [Step name]
Result: [audience-language result]
Covers: [3-5 points]

Video 2 - [Step name]
Result: [audience-language result]
Covers: [3-5 points]

(continue for all 5)
```

Ask: "Does this look right? Is the arc clear - does each video lead naturally into the next?"

STOP - confirm before continuing.

## Step 4 - Video 1 framing

Every video in the series teaches one step of the method AND builds toward the overall transformation. Video 1 carries a slightly heavier framing load because it's the entry point - first-time viewers land here and need to understand the whole journey they're being invited into.

Video 1 needs to:
1. **Set up the overall transformation** - paint the picture of the desirable result the full series delivers. Use the user's own story where it fits to pull people in.
2. **Teach step 1 of the method in full** - real, actionable value on the first step. Not just a tease.
3. **Set up video 2** - the rehook into the next step naturally.

the creator's example: her transformation series video 1 was titled "launch your web design business in 2024 (full guide)." The title sets up the overall transformation (launching a web design business). The video taught step 1 of her method (positioning) in full. Then it bridged into video 2.

If the Video 1 outline doesn't reflect both the overall framing AND step 1's value, revise it before moving to titles.

---

## Step 4b - CTA placement across the series

The series CTA is the channel CTA from `core_channel-positioning.md`. Where it lands across the 5 videos depends on what's most naturally relevant to the user.

The principle: place the CTA where the user is naturally referencing the thing the offer delivers. Don't force it into all 5 videos. Don't reserve it for the last video. Drop it where it makes sense.

the creator's example: her offer is templates. Videos 4 and 5 were about client process and web design process - she was literally showing the templates she uses. So the CTA ("grab the templates below") landed naturally in those videos. Videos 1-3 funnelled toward those offers without explicitly mentioning the product.

Walk through the 5 videos and decide:
- **Which videos naturally reference or demonstrate the thing the offer delivers?** Those are the CTA videos.
- **Which videos build trust and value but don't have a natural CTA moment?** Those don't need a hard CTA - they're funnelling viewers toward the videos that do.

Note the CTA placement decision in the series overview before moving to titles.

## Step 5 - Generate titles and thumbnail phrases

For each video, generate:
- 2-3 directional title options (using the rules from /youtube-title - lowercase, no emojis, audience-language result over topic name)
- 1 thumbnail phrase (3-5 words that would go on the thumbnail - what the title doesn't say)

These are DIRECTIONAL - enough to lock in the series arc. When the user goes to script each individual video via /youtube-script, /youtube-title runs in full with the radar and produces the proper 10 titles + 5 thumbnail phrases for A/B testing. Don't try to do that here - this is the planning phase.

Titles must:
- Be lowercase
- Have no emojis
- Reflect the core result in the audience's own language
- Be clear on what it is AND aspirational on the result

Thumbnail phrases must:
- Be 3-5 words maximum
- Communicate something NOT in the title (a result, a credibility signal, or an "I haven't seen this" angle)
- Never repeat title language

This skill does NOT produce visual thumbnail descriptions or design direction. Visual thumbnail generation is /youtube-thumbnail (separate skill). This skill stops at the words.

Present all 5 sets together:

```
**Video 1 - [Step name]**
Title options:
- [title]
- [title]
- [title]
Thumbnail phrase: [3-5 words]

**Video 2 - [Step name]**
[Repeat]

(continue for all 5)
```

STOP - confirm title and thumbnail phrase choices for each video before saving.

## Rules

- Every video maps to ONE step in core IP and DELIVERS the full value of that step. No "video 1 is the hook, video 5 is the result" framing - that's wrong.
- Video 1 carries extra framing load (overall transformation + step 1) but still teaches step 1 in full.
- CTA placement is contextual, not formulaic. Drop the CTA where the offer is naturally relevant. Some videos have a CTA, some don't - that's correct.
- Goal: give everything away free, sell the implementation. The CTA earns its place by being the natural next step from what was just taught.
- Use judgment to shape the series to the user's specific channel (their content style, delivery style, offer, method). Don't apply a template blindly.
- The result of the full series must match the channel transformation.
- The CTA is the channel CTA from `core_channel-positioning.md`.
- Never invent content points - only what comes from the user's IP and POV.
- Results in the AUDIENCE'S language, not the marketing version.
- Titles lowercase, no emojis.
- Never use - (em dash). Use - instead.
- Respect every STOP condition.
