# YouTube Script Intro

The intro must hit the **3 Cs** in the first 30 seconds (or first 3 sentences). The 3 Cs are the *outcome* the intro must achieve. The 5-part framework is one *delivery vehicle*, not the only one. Voice is non-negotiable - the intro must sound like the user, not a generic YouTube template.

**Before writing anything, check `EXAMPLES.md` in this skill folder if it exists.** It contains intros the user has approved word-for-word. Model new intros after the closest-fit example. Examples override any framework template they conflict with.

## The 3 Cs (must all land within first 30 seconds)

The 3 Cs are the test the first 30 seconds must pass. If a viewer lands on the video and watches the first 30 seconds, they must walk away knowing all three:

| C | Question it answers | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Clarity** | What is this video about, who is it for, and what will I be able to do after watching? | Title + transformation + audience |
| **Credibility** | Why should I listen to you on this? | A real proof point from the user's experience |
| **Curiosity** | What's the hook that keeps me watching? An open loop, a contrarian claim, a counter-intuitive promise, or a slightly different angle. | Contrarian flip + plan tease |

**Before presenting any intro, run the 3 Cs check. If any C is weak or missing, rewrite.**

## The 5 parts (one way to deliver the 3 Cs)

The 5-part framework is one structure. It's not the only structure. The 3 Cs are what must land - the parts below are a tested vehicle for getting them across.

Parts can be embedded in narrative flow. They don't have to appear as labelled boxes in the script. Each part can be 1-3 sentences. Skip a part if a different structure delivers the 3 Cs better for this specific video.

| # | Part | Job | Delivers |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Context** | Establish what the video is about, who it's for, and the outcome. | Clarity |
| 2 | **Baseline belief** | Name what most viewers currently believe or have been told. | Curiosity (sets up the flip) |
| 3 | **Contrarian flip** | Reframe with the user's actual insight. The hook. | Curiosity |
| 4 | **Proof** | Specific result, number, named experience, or timeframe. | Credibility |
| 5 | **Plan** | What they'll walk away with and why to stay. | Clarity (reinforces) |

### Sentence frameworks per part

Use these as starting points, not as a strict template. Voice overrides them.

**Context:**
- "In this video I'm gonna share the [framework/system] I used to [desirable result] in [timeframe]."
- "In this video I want to show you how I went from [before] to [after]."

**Baseline belief** (pick the framing that fits):
- External: "Most people think [common belief]."
- Personal: "I used to think [former belief]."
- Industry: "If you ask most [people in your niche], they'll tell you [belief] - but here's what they're missing."

**Contrarian flip:**
- "But the truth is, [unexpected insight]."
- "But I was able to [result] with [contrarian method]."
- "The problem with that approach is [why it fails]."

**Proof:**
- "I know because I [result] in [specific timeframe]."
- "I know because this is the same method I used to [result]."

**Plan:**
- "By the end, you'll know exactly how to [specific capability] so you never have to [pain] again."
- "By the end of this video, you'll have the full [framework] for [outcome]."

**CRITICAL:** Plan is not Context restated. Context = what the video IS. Plan = what the viewer WALKS AWAY ABLE TO DO. If your Plan draft is just Context with "by the end, you'll know..." prepended, rewrite it.

## Voice-first opening rules

The intro must sound like the user. Generic YouTube templates fail this test. Read `core_voice-style.md` first - the user's own opening patterns there override the templates below.

**The principle:** the first sentence must be the most compelling, result-driven sentence available for this specific video AND it must match the expectation the title sets. The viewer just clicked on a specific promise - the first sentence should land directly inside that promise, not pivot somewhere else.

Lead with what's strongest within the boundary the title created. Don't lead with what's expected. Don't lead with something that breaks the title's promise.

Strong opening options - pick whichever is most compelling for this video:

1. **Personal admission of resistance or struggle:** "For the longest time I refused to do X..." / "I swore I would never..."
2. **Credibility-forward statement:** "I have built a [thing] and [result]..." (only if the user has the proof)
3. **Direct tension or contradiction:** "Picking a niche can be both the best and worst thing for your business."
4. **Concrete result + personal anchor:** "I have made [result]. But [the hard part]..."
5. **Story-as-proof opening:** A short story that demonstrates the standard the user holds, the effort or cost, the result, and the realisation that flips into the contrarian view.
6. **Direct context statement:** "In this video, I'm going to show you how to [specific result]." This works as the first sentence when the result itself is the hook - when the promise is so specific and so tied to a real outcome that stating it plainly is more compelling than any setup.

The choice between these is about what's strongest for this video, not about following a default. If a personal story or admission lands harder than "in this video..." use that. If the cleanest, most compelling thing is the direct promise, lead with that.

**Never:**
- No greeting, no "welcome back," no "hey guys," no thank-yous
- No hollow hook questions ("Have you ever wondered...?")
- No "you guys" - talk to one person

## Story-as-proof pattern

When the topic involves the user's own track record or transformation, the strongest pattern is a story that doubles as proof:

1. The standard the user held (or still holds)
2. The effort, cost, or commitment they put in
3. The result that came from it
4. The standard reinforced or shifted
5. The admission of what changed
6. The contrarian flip that this story proves

Length flex: story-driven intros can run to 100 seconds if the story IS the credibility. Do not force-cut a story-as-proof intro to fit a strict 60-second cap.

## How to run the skill

Two modes:

### Mode A - Walk through (default)

If the user wants to build the intro collaboratively:

1. Confirm required inputs (see "When to stop and ask" below)
2. Walk through the 5 parts one at a time, in order
3. For each part: name its job, offer 1-2 drafts pulled from voice style + assets, ask the user to pick or tweak, STOP and wait
4. Once all 5 are locked, present the final intro per the output format below

### Mode B - One-shot draft

If the user provides full context up front and wants a draft to react to:

1. Read all required files
2. Pick the opening pattern that best fits the video (from voice style or the 5 patterns above)
3. Draft the intro as a flowing narrative (not labelled boxes)
4. Run the 3 Cs check before presenting
5. Present per the output format below

Always present in this exact format:

```
**3 Cs check:**

- Credibility: [one line - what makes this trustworthy]

**5-part breakdown:**
- Context: [the sentence(s) that do this job]
- Baseline belief: [the sentence(s) that do this job]
- Contrarian flip: [the sentence(s) that do this job]
- Proof: [the sentence(s) that do this job]
- Plan: [the sentence(s) that do this job]
```

Then, on a new line, list any gaps that need user input.

## When to stop and ask

Stop and ask the user before writing if any of these are missing:

- Video title or working title
- The transformation (before / after for the viewer)
- The baseline belief (what the viewer currently thinks)
- The contrarian flip (what the user actually believes)
- A real proof point from the user's own experience

If multiple are missing, ask in one batched message. Never make up proof.

---

## Hard rules

1. **3 Cs are the test, not the 5 parts.** If the script delivers all 3 Cs in 30 seconds with a different structure, that's fine. The 5 parts exist to help deliver the 3 Cs - they are not the goal.
2. **Voice overrides framework.** If the framework's sentence template collides with the user's natural opening, rewrite using their opening pattern.
3. **Never invent proof.** No made-up numbers, dates, client names, or results. If the user hasn't given a proof point and none exists in `05_Assets/`, stop and ask.
4. **Never use em dashes.** Use hyphens.
5. **Total length:** 30-90 seconds spoken. Story-driven intros can extend to 100 seconds.
6. **Sentence rhythm:** vary sentence length. Don't write five short sentences in a row or five long. Use fragments deliberately for emphasis.
7. **Talk to one person.** No "you guys."

## Failure modes - self-check before presenting

- [ ] All 3 Cs land in the first 30 seconds (Clarity, Credibility, Curiosity)
- [ ] First sentence is the most compelling, result-driven option for THIS video (not a default opener)
- [ ] First sentence matches the expectation the title creates - lands inside the title's promise, doesn't pivot
- [ ] No em dashes anywhere
- [ ] No invented numbers, names, or results
- [ ] No greeting, no "welcome back," no hollow hook question
- [ ] Could only be this user - if you could lift this and put it on any other channel, rewrite
- [ ] Variation in sentence length
- [ ] Connects to title and video promise
- [ ] Spoken length 30-90 seconds (up to 100 if story-driven)
- [ ] Plan names viewer capability, not just video content
- [ ] Proof is grounded in the user's own experience

If any check fails, rewrite before presenting.
