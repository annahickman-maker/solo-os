# YouTube Script Context

The context section is what comes between the intro and the first value section. Its purpose varies based on the video - sometimes it's a full mindset shift to overcome a major objection, sometimes it's just light additional setup, and sometimes it's not needed at all because the intro already did the work.

The job is to use judgment first - decide what this specific video needs - then run the appropriate flow. Forcing every video through a heavy 6-beat mindset shift is wrong. So is skipping it without thinking.

When the section IS needed, the strongest version anchors to a personal story from the user that demonstrates the AHA. Generic "you might be doing this right now" framing is the fallback, not the default.

## Step 1 - Judgment call: which mode does this video need?

**Default goal: Mode C (full mindset shift, story-anchored).** This is the strongest version of the section. It builds up to the value, emphasises why the rest of the video matters, and gets the viewer genuinely excited or relieved before any tactical content lands. Most videos benefit from this when the user has a story and a clear mindset shift to drive home.

The other modes exist for when Mode C isn't the right fit:

- **Mode B (light setup)** is the fallback when the user doesn't have a personal story, isn't sure what the mindset shift is, or genuinely just needs to set additional context without overcoming a deep objection.
- **Mode A (skip)** is for short, tutorial-style videos where mindset work isn't relevant - someone wants to know how to do a specific thing, the value IS the video, and forcing a mindset section would slow it down.

### How to decide

Look at the locked intro and the video's nature. Ask:

1. **Is this a short tutorial / how-to video where the audience just wants the answer?**
   → Mode A. Skip the section, go straight to value.
   
2. **Does the user have a personal story or lived experience that demonstrates the AHA, AND is there a clear belief/objection to overcome?**
   → Mode C. This is the default when the conditions are met - run with it.
   
3. **Is there context the viewer needs before the value will land, but no clear personal story or no big mindset shift to make?**
   → Mode B. Light setup, 30-60 seconds, no story required.

### Tell the user the call

> "Recommendation: Mode [A/B/C].
>
> [Mode A] This looks like a short tutorial-style video - I'd skip the context section and go straight from intro to value. Want to run with that?"
>
> [Mode B] You don't seem to have a clear personal story or a major objection to overcome here, so I'd run a light setup (30-60s) instead of a full mindset shift. Sound right?"
>
> [Mode C - default when conditions are met] You've got a real story and a clear mindset shift to make. Let's run a full mindset shift section anchored to your [story name] - 60-120s, building up to why the rest of the video matters."

Wait for confirmation before proceeding.

## Mode B - Light setup (3 beats, 30-60 seconds)

Each beat is 1-2 sentences. Beats flow naturally, not labelled in the script.

### 1. Set the stage
Acknowledge what the viewer is bringing into this video - the situation, the doubt, or the missing piece.
- "So before I get into [the value], here's the thing you need to know about [topic]..."
- "The reason this works comes down to [single concept]..."

### 2. Frame why this matters
Set up the stakes or the underlying principle. Why is what's coming actually going to land?
- "If you don't get this right, [consequence]..."
- "This is the difference between [A] and [B]..."

### 3. Bridge into value
Hand off into the first value section.
- "And so the first thing you need to do is [first value point]..."
- "Let's start with [value 1 topic]..."

## Mode C - Full mindset shift (6 beats, 60-120 seconds)

Strongly prefer using a personal story from the user as the spine of beats 1, 3, and 5. If the user has a story in `05_Assets/Stories/` that demonstrates the AHA, use it. If not, ASK the user before falling back to generic mirroring - their story is much more powerful than "you might be doing this right now."

### Example of a story-anchored mindset shift (illustrating the pattern)

> "When I first started, I was scared to charge more or niche down because I thought I was cutting off potential clients. I kept my prices low and stayed broad on purpose, thinking it was the safer move.
>
> The problem with that approach is it actually attracts everyone who's not a fit - and repels the people who ARE.
>
> The moment it changed for me was when I niched down to one specific kind of client and tripled my price - and within two weeks I had three perfect-fit clients reach out who were happy to pay it.
>
> So instead of trying to be for everyone, I started narrowing aggressively.
>
> What I realised is that the riskier-feeling move - charging more, getting more specific - is actually the easier path to attracting the right people. Being broad and cheap is the hard version. Most people get this backwards.
>
> So in this video I'm gonna walk you through how to do this for your own business..."

That's the shape - lived experience, the moment of realisation, the shift, why it works better, bridge into value.

### The 6 beats

#### 1. Mirror their situation (anchored to your story when possible)
Open by reflecting back exactly where the viewer is - ideally through your own past experience.
- Best version: "When I first started, I was [doing the same thing the viewer is doing now], because I thought [the same belief the viewer holds]."
- Fallback if no story fits: "You're probably [doing X right now] because you think [Y]."
- Goal: viewer feels completely seen and connected to the user's experience.

#### 2. Name what's not working and why
Show the LOGIC of the broken approach - it makes sense on the surface. Then explain why it leads to the wrong place.
- Not "you're wrong"
- More "here's why this feels like the right move but actually backfires"
- Make the trap visible.

#### 3. The AHA - what changed (anchored to a specific moment)
This is the turn. Specific moment, specific realisation, specific story.
- "The moment it changed for me was when..."
- "I'll never forget the day I [specific event]..."
- Vague AHAs ("I just realised one day...") fail. The AHA must have a clear specific anchor.

#### 4. What you started doing instead
One sentence. Name the shift, don't teach the tactical yet.
- "So instead of [old thing], I started [new thing]."

#### 5. Why this works better - build it up
Spend the most time here. Sell why this shift matters before getting into the tactical value. This is what creates buy-in.
- Use proof, results, or how it changed the day-to-day
- Goal: viewer should feel genuine relief or excitement that there's a better way - before any teaching has happened

#### 6. Bridge into value
Hand off into the first value section.
- "So in this video, I'm gonna walk you through how to do this for your own [thing]..."
- Or "And the way you actually do this comes down to [first value point]..."

## Required inputs (when running Mode B or Mode C)

Confirm before drafting. If missing, ask.

- The video's transformation (before/after for the viewer)
- The locked intro (especially baseline belief + contrarian flip)
- For Mode C: a personal story or experience that demonstrates the AHA (from `05_Assets/Stories/` or user input - never invented). If no story exists and the AHA is real, ask the user to share it before proceeding.
- The first value point (so the bridge is specific)

---

## How to run the skill

1. Run Step 1 - judgment call. Tell the user which mode you're recommending and why. Wait for confirmation.
2. If Mode A (skip), hand off.
3. If Mode B or C, ask for any missing required inputs.
4. **Default mode for B and C:** walk through one beat at a time, conversational. For each beat: name its job, offer 1-2 drafts pulled from voice style + assets, ask the user to pick or tweak, STOP and wait. Once locked, move to the next.
5. **One-shot draft mode (if user prefers):** read all files, draft the full section, run the self-check, present.

## Hard rules

1. **Default to Mode C when the conditions are met.** The full mindset shift, story-anchored, is the strongest version of this section - it's what you're working toward. Drop to Mode B or A only when the conditions for C aren't there (no story, no clear shift, or short tutorial-style video).
2. **Personal story is the default, not the fallback.** Generic "you might be feeling X" is the backup when no story exists. If the user has a relevant story, use it.
3. **Voice overrides framework.** Match `core_voice-style.md`.
4. **Never invent stories, proof, or AHA moments.** If no real story fits, drop to Mode B - don't fabricate one to keep Mode C.
5. **Never use em dashes.** Use hyphens.
6. **Length matches the mode.** Mode B: 30-60 seconds. Mode C: 60-120 seconds. If a section is creeping past those bounds, you're either teaching tactically (don't) or repeating the intro (don't).
7. **No tactical teaching in this section.** That's the value sections. The context sets up; it doesn't teach.

## Failure modes - self-check before presenting

- [ ] You ran Step 1 and made a deliberate mode choice based on whether Mode C's conditions were met (story + clear shift + not a short tutorial)
- [ ] If running Mode C: at least one beat is anchored to a real personal story (or the user explicitly provided one)
- [ ] AHA is specific - moment, decision, realisation - not vague ("I just realised one day")
- [ ] No tactical content - positioning only
- [ ] Length matches mode (B: 30-60s, C: 60-120s)
- [ ] Voice matches `core_voice-style.md`
- [ ] No invented stories
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] Bridges naturally into the named first value point

If any check fails, rewrite before presenting.
