# YouTube Ideas

Takes a raw idea (one line is enough) and turns it into a packaged video concept that the audience will actually click on.

The most important job: bridge between **what the user wants to teach** (often tactical, granular) and **what the audience actually wants** (a desirable result). These are usually different. Nobody wakes up wanting to learn how to build a client portal - they wake up wanting to charge more for their web design projects. The job here is to connect the teaching to the result.

---

## Before you start

Open warm and human - lead with something like "Hey [first name], let's sharpen this idea." Then load your context quietly in the background. Never narrate file reads or paths; the person just sees the conversation.

Read your foundation (set up in Solo OS onboarding):

- `01_Core/core_positioning.md` - who you help and the transformation
- `01_Core/core_audience.md` - your audience
- `01_Core/core_ip.md` - your core method, to see what this idea connects to

Then load your content focus avatar - the specific person this video is for. Read `content_focus_avatar` from `00_System/state.md` (it's a path to an avatar file), then read that avatar in `05_Assets/Avatars/`. It's the one you've selected on your Content page. If none is set, fall back to `core_audience.md`.

If your core files aren't set up yet, stop and say: run /solopreneur-onboarding first.

## The raw idea

One line is enough. If they'd rather start from something they've already jotted down, offer what's in their idea bank.

---

## Step 1 - Understand the raw idea

If anything is unclear, ask. If the idea is one line, that's fine - proceed.

Check internally:
- Does this connect back to the user's core method in `core_ip.md`?
- If yes, note which stage of the method it connects to.
- If no, flag it: "This doesn't connect directly to your core method. That's not a reason to drop it, but it's worth knowing - it may attract a different audience."

---

## Step 2 - Bridge to a desirable audience result

The audience clicks on videos framed around what they WANT, not on whatever the user wants to teach. Sometimes those are the same thing. Sometimes they're not. This step uses judgment to figure out which case applies, and only does the bridging work when it's needed.

Your `core_positioning.md` (the transformation, where you're taking people) and your focus avatar (their before/after, what they're really after) tell you what the audience actually wants.

### First - judgment call

Ask yourself (silently, not the user):
> "Is this raw idea ALREADY framed around something the audience is directly searching for or wanting an answer to? Or is it a tactical/granular thing that needs to be connected to a higher-level desirable result?"

**Examples of ideas that are ALREADY the desirable result** (no bridge needed):
- "How to charge more for web design" - audience is directly searching for this
- "How to land your first high-ticket client" - directly desirable
- "How I sold $50K of digital products" - directly desirable, the result IS the topic
- "5 mistakes that killed my first launch" - audience wants to avoid this exact pain

**Examples of ideas that need bridging** (tactical → result):
- "How to build a client portal" → bridge to "how to charge more / elevated client experience"
- "How I use Notion for my business" → bridge to whatever result the system delivers (faster project delivery, more clients, etc)
- "My morning routine" → bridge to whatever result the routine produces (more output, better decisions, etc)

### If the idea is ALREADY the desirable result

Confirm with the user:
> "This idea is already framed around something your audience wants - [restate the desirable result]. We don't need to bridge it to anything else; the topic IS the result."

Note this in the package and move directly to Step 3.

### If the idea needs bridging

Ask:
> "Your audience isn't waking up looking to learn [the tactical thing in your raw idea]. They're waking up wanting [a desirable result from your channel positioning]. So before we shape this into a video - what's the desirable audience result this idea actually delivers?
>
> For example: if your idea is 'how to build a client portal,' nobody is searching for that. But people ARE searching for 'how to charge more for web design.' A client portal is part of an elevated client experience that justifies higher prices. So the video isn't really about client portals - it's about charging more, with the portal as the mechanism.
>
> What's the version of that for your idea?"

Push the user to articulate:
- The desirable result their audience wants (cross-check with `core_audience.md` and the channel's "from A to B" section)
- The connection: how does what you want to teach contribute to that result?
- The reframing: how would the video be framed around the result, not the teaching?

If the user genuinely can't make a clean bridge, flag it directly:
> "I'm not seeing a strong link between [the raw idea] and [a desirable result your audience wants]. Two options: either we find a different framing that connects it, or this idea might land better with a different audience than your channel's. Which do you want to do?"

STOP - confirm the bridge before continuing.

### Edge case - genuinely useful but not directly desirable

Some ideas are valuable to the audience but the audience doesn't know they want them yet. These are "you don't know you need this until I show you" videos. They can work but they're harder to get the click. If this is the case, flag it:

> "This is a 'you don't know you need this' kind of video - your audience won't be searching for it, but it'll be valuable once they watch. These can work, but the title and thumbnail will need to do extra work to get the click. Want to proceed and lean into a strong angle/hook to compensate?"

If yes, treat it as a bridging case but be honest in the package note about the discovery challenge.

---

## Step 3 - Find the specific angle

Now compress to the specific POV or insight that makes this video worth watching - the thing that's yours, not generic.

Ask:
> "What's the specific thing you believe about this that most people don't? What's the angle that makes your take on this different from the 50 other videos on the same topic?"

If they're stuck, push:
> "Imagine two videos with the same topic and the same desirable result framing. One is generic advice anyone could give. One is yours. What makes yours different - what do you know about this that someone else wouldn't?"

A good angle should make the viewer feel like they're getting something they couldn't get elsewhere.

STOP - confirm the angle before continuing.

---

## Step 4 - Define the transformation

Ask:
> "What does the viewer walk away able to do or think differently after watching this? Not what you'll cover - what changes for them?"

Push for a clean before/after framed around the desirable result from Step 2:
- **Before:** [what they think or do now - the stuck/struggling state]
- **After:** [what they think or do after watching - one step closer to the desirable result]

The transformation should naturally lead toward the desirable result. If it doesn't, the bridge in Step 2 wasn't strong enough - go back.

STOP - confirm before continuing.

---

## Step 5 - The click test

Use this exact question to validate the framing:

> "If your audience was scrolling YouTube and saw this video framed around [the desirable result] with the angle [the specific POV] - would they be interested? Would they want to click on it? Do they know it's for them?"

If the answer is hesitant or uncertain, push:
> "Where's the gap? Is the result not actually desirable enough? Is the angle not clear enough? Or is the audience not going to recognise themselves in the framing?"

Then check supporting demand signals:
> "Have you seen questions about this in your community, comments on your videos, DMs, or search results? What tells you people are actually looking for this?"

If they have evidence, note it - it becomes proof in the title and script.

If they don't, flag it:
> "Worth checking before you script this. Search the topic on YouTube and see what comes up. If there are videos with strong view counts relative to the channel size, demand is there."

STOP - confirm the click test passes before packaging.

---

## Step 6 - Package it

Combine everything into a packaged concept:

```
**Video concept**

- **Raw idea (what you want to teach):** [original idea]
- **Desirable audience result (what they want):** [the result from Step 2]
- **Bridge (how the teaching delivers the result):** [the connection]
- **Angle (your unique POV):** [from Step 3]
- **Transformation:** [before → after]
- **Click test:** [why the audience will click]
- **Demand signal:** [evidence people want this, if any]
- **Working title direction:** [1-2 sentence direction for the title - framed around the desirable result, not the teaching]
- **Teaching points:** [3-5 bullet points - what the video will cover]
- **Connects to:** [which stage of the core method this relates to, or "not directly tied to core method"]
```

Present and ask:
> "Does this feel like the right video? Is the framing around [the desirable result] strong enough that your audience would click? Is the angle sharp enough that someone who's seen 10 videos on this topic would feel like yours is different?"

STOP - confirm before closing.

---

## Step 7 - Hand off

Once confirmed:

> "This is ready. Take this into /youtube-title to write your title (the title generator will frame around the desirable result), then into /youtube-script to build the outline and intro."

If they want to keep this concept, add it to their YouTube queue so it shows up on the dashboard. Save it as a new video project at `04_Channel/04_Projects/project_<slug>.md` (slug = 2-3 words from the working title), with this frontmatter:

    ---
    id: <slug>
    type: video
    title: <working title>
    status: idea
    queued: true
    created: '<today, YYYY-MM-DD>'
    ---

Put the packaged concept in the body. The `status: idea` + `queued: true` pair is what makes it show up in the YouTube queue on your Content page.

Create a brand-new per-row file with its own `project_<slug>.md` name. Do NOT append the concept to `_video-queue.md` or any other list/markdown file - the dashboard reads individual `type: video` files, so a list entry will never appear in the queue.

---

## Rules

- Never package a vague idea - push for specificity first.
- Never let the framing stay tactical when it should be result-driven. The audience clicks on results.
- Never invent demand signals, audience desires, or audience traits.
- Pull from your core files and your focus avatar for context - especially the transformation and who this video is for.
- Never use - (em dash). Use - instead.
- Respect every STOP condition.
