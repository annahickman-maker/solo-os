# Methodology: concept → system → execution

The taste this skill teaches is not a style. It is a **method**. A method survives across brands; a style imposes a sameness that violates the whole point. The reference work lives in this exact distinction: every brand looks unmistakably its own, but the underlying process is identical.

## The three-phase loop

### Phase 1 - Catch the bug

Before anything visual, become a superfan of the brand. Read everything the user has given you. Watch the founder's interview if there is one. Scroll the customer reviews. Look at the literal product, the literal place it gets used, the literal hand that uses it.

The output of this phase is **not a moodboard**. It is the **conceptual seed** - one to three sentences naming the central metaphor or idea. If you cannot write the seed, you have not caught the bug yet, and any design you produce will be a guess.

Common places the seed hides:
- The founder's origin story (one specific scene, not the resume)
- The strongest piece of customer language (one phrase that keeps repeating)
- The literal product, examined as an object (what does it weigh, what does it sound like, what does it smell like)
- The cultural reference the user keeps gravitating toward (a film, a magazine, a piece of music, a place)

A good seed names a tension or a metaphor. Not adjectives. "A river carving its own path" is good. "Premium and trustworthy" is not.

### Phase 2 - Map the gap

With the seed in hand, map the visual world the brand will live in. This is the brief.

For every system - typography, color, imagery, layout, copy - answer:
- What does the seed dictate here?
- What does the category default to, and where do we deliberately deviate?
- What is the one move that makes this brand instantly recognizable?

The system is **disciplined**, not elaborate. A two-typeface pairing, a five-color palette with a clear ratio, a single image direction with three to five prompt variants, a grid with a stated rhythm, a voice with a tagline and three sample sentences. That is enough to design fifty pages.

If the system needs more than that to express the seed, the seed is fuzzy. Go back to phase 1.

### Phase 3 - Design the movement

Now implement. The implementation rule is: **every decision traces back to the seed, through the system, into the page**. If a decision does not trace, cut it.

Restraint is the operating mode. Editorial impact comes from removal:
- One photograph carrying a section, not three.
- One headline at 7vw, not three at 2rem.
- One accent color on the page in two places, not four colors on every component.
- One long paragraph in narrow measure, not five short paragraphs in three columns.

When in doubt, scale up the typography, increase the white space, and remove a section. You will rarely be wrong.

## The discipline that makes this work

The hardest part of this method is not finding the seed. It is **refusing to add things that do not trace**. Every brand site under construction will, at some point, want a "trust badges" row, a "featured in" logo strip, a "three pillars" block, a "meet the team" grid. These are defaults that solve nothing. Some brands need them. Most do not. Default to no, then add only if the seed demands it.

The taste is the discipline. Hold it.

## How this method maps to the workflow in SKILL.md

| Workflow step | Phase |
|---|---|
| 1. Load references | preparation |
| 2. Find the conceptual seed | Phase 1 (Catch the bug) |
| 3. Fill out the brand brief, get approval | Phase 2 (Map the gap) |

| 5. Implement against the brief | Phase 3 (Design the movement) |
