# Case 02 - A performance beauty brand built around frosted glass and condensation

A makeup brand for women who actually sweat. The category default is glossy, pink-and-gold, beauty-counter polish. The rebrand pushed the opposite direction: aestheticize the heat and effort that performance creates, instead of pretending the customer never breaks a sweat.

## The conceptual seed

> Frosted glass and a single bead of condensation rolling down the side. The brand celebrates the heat, sweat, and flush of a real workout - the moisture is the proof, not a problem to hide.

The seed turns a category negative ("makeup that runs") into a category signature ("makeup made for the sweat"). Every visual decision flows from condensation: the bottle is literal frosted glass, the photography emphasizes flushed skin and damp hair, the palette pulls from the sapphire of the bottle and the blush of exertion.

## Trace map

| Decision | Trace |
|---|---|
| Frosted-glass spray bottle as primary product | The seed made literal |
| Sapphire blue accent `#2B5A8E` | The actual color of the glass |
| Blush accent `#E8A8A0` | Skin flushed from heat - the proof |
| Linen `#E8DDD0` neutral background | Sun-warmed, not clinical-white |
| Photography of women mid-motion, hair damp, cheeks pink | Subjects who have actually been doing the thing |
| Hard sun late-afternoon lighting | The light of an actual sweat |
| Tagline "Why isn't there makeup made for women who sweat?" | The category-disrupting question |
| Body voice: "Just effective, breathable, real-life wear" | Anti-glossy, anti-perfection |

## Typography

- **Display**: a contemporary geometric sans (could be GT America, Söhne, or for free-tier Geist) - clean, athletic-feeling, no editorial-serif softness
- **Body**: same family, lighter weight, comfortable size
- **Wordmark**: subtly customized version of the display face, slightly stretched
- One italicized accent serif word in the hero ("real") for warmth contrast

This is the **sharp grotesk + italic serif accent** archetype from typography.md, used because the seed has more athletic grit than literary softness.

## Imagery direction

Two lighting modes are used together because the brand has two registers: hard sun for action shots (a runner on a beach, a woman climbing rocks, golden hour but harsher), studio-with-controlled-spill for product shots (the bottle on warmed concrete, condensation visible).

Subject library: women in their actual sport (running, climbing, surfing - not "fitness studio" stock), close-up of dewy skin, the bottle in real environments (gym bag, beach towel, gravel path). Always wet, always real.

What's deliberately avoided: the studio "beauty shot" with the model's face perfect and centered. That is the entire category, and the brand is the inverse.

## Layout posture

- Hero: a hard-sun action shot, woman from behind running on a coastal road, sweat visible. Headline overlaid lower-left in deep ink: a question, not a statement.
- Section 1: the brand answer, in narrow measure, with the question's language inverted.
- Section 2: asymmetric layout - product shot left (the bottle on warmed concrete), copy right with eyebrow MADE FOR.
- Section 3: full-bleed contact-sheet of 5 images, all in matching golden-hour grade.
- Section 4: a single pull quote in oversized type ("Sweat is not the failure. Pretending you don't is.") - a single sentence.
- CTA: deep ink button on linen, "Find your shade."

## Copy samples

- Hero: "Why isn't there makeup made for women who sweat?"
- Thesis: "Because the rest of the category was made for the photo, not the day. We built this for the day."
- Eyebrow + section: "MADE FOR / The 6am run. The 9am call. The 4pm school pickup. All without reapplying."

## Lessons

- The most powerful seeds **invert a category negative**. Stock beauty photography pretends sweat doesn't exist. This brand makes sweat the proof.
- The bottle's literal color became the accent. This is allowed when the product is a hero. It is not allowed when "we want the brand to feel premium so let's make everything black and gold" - that is not a trace, it is a vibe.
- Sharp typography is correct here precisely because the brand is athletic, not literary. Editorial serif would have softened the brand into the same category sameness it is rejecting.
- One question on the hero is more confident than three benefits. The reader leans in to answer.
