# Case 01 - A wellness brand built around a flowing-water metaphor

A women's integrative health brand. Pre-rebrand, the name and identity were generic - clinical, blue-and-white, indistinguishable from a hundred other "wellness" companies. The rename and rebuild traced a single conceptual seed all the way through to image grade.

## The conceptual seed

> A river carving its own path. Healing as non-linear forward motion. The brand name itself fuses river + well: grounded but fluid, scientific but soulful.

The seed gives permission for the entire visual world: photography of water and stone, color drawn from sun-on-water and moss, copy that uses motion verbs ("flow," "return," "move"), section labels that feel quietly authoritative.

## Trace map (every visual decision back to the seed)

| Decision | Trace |
|---|---|
| Name fusing river-noun + well-noun | The seed itself |
| Off-white cream `#F4EFE6` as 80% of the page | River-bed light, water-on-pale-stone |
| Sage `#7A9B7F` as primary accent | The vegetated bank, the moss the river runs over |
| Mustard `#D4A574` as secondary accent | Sunlight breaking on water surface |
| Photography of bare feet on river stones, hands in moss | The seed made literal |
| Editorial serif display + clean sans body | Editorial seriousness without clinical coldness |
| Body copy in narrow measure (56ch) | Reads like a magazine essay, not a clinic pamphlet |
| Tagline "Flow Back To You" | Verb of the seed + second-person return-to-self |
| Section labels OVERVIEW / APPROACH / SERVICES | The brand's authority register |

## Typography

- **Display**: an editorial serif with high contrast (Fraunces variable, with `opsz` axis cranked at large sizes for elegance)
- **Body**: Inter at 17px / 1.6 line-height, narrow measure
- **Eyebrows**: Inter Medium, ALL CAPS, +0.14em tracking
- One italicized word per hero ("Flow") to introduce the motion language

## Color (with usage ratio)

```
--bg-cream:    #F4EFE6   /* 75% of page */
--bg-deep:     #1A1A1A   /* 8% - section backgrounds, body */
--accent-sage: #7A9B7F   /* 10% - logo, single section, accents */
--accent-sun:  #D4A574   /* 5% - one CTA per section, single underline */
--hairline:    rgba(26,26,26,0.12)
```

## Imagery direction

Six lighting modes were ruled out; one was chosen: golden-hour raking light with Kodak Portra 400 grade. Every photograph in the brand uses this combination. That single discipline is what gives the brand its visual coherence.

Subject library: bare feet in shallow water, a hand on river stones, a woman walking through tall grass, a wide environmental of a river bend at dusk. No faces in close-up. No model-centric portraits. The brand is about the place, not the person.

## Layout posture

- Hero: full-bleed photograph, full viewport height. Headline placed lower-left third, oversized italic serif "Flow Back To You" in cream.
- Section 1 (after hero): a single paragraph in narrow measure, centered, brand thesis. `--space-7` of breathing room above and below.
- Section 2: asymmetric image + text, photograph 8 columns left, paragraph 4 columns right with eyebrow APPROACH above.
- Section 3: pull-quote section, a single sentence in italic serif at 4rem, max-width 22ch.
- Section 4: contact-sheet row of 5 small images, all in matching grade.
- CTA: single button, deep-ink-on-cream, with the mustard accent showing up as a thin underline beneath the button text only.

## Copy samples

- Hero: "Flow back to you."
- Thesis: "Healing is not linear, but always moving forward. Like a river that finds its path around what tries to stop it, the body knows where it is going - if we let it."
- Eyebrow + section: "APPROACH / Editorial meets organic. Minimal meets warm."

## What made the brand work

- The seed was not "premium and trustworthy." It was a metaphor that could generate images, palette, and copy without any further intervention.
- The discipline of one lighting mode across all photography is what reads as "brand" rather than "stock photos."
- The accent colors come from the metaphor, not from a "wellness color theory" exercise.
- The site refuses to add a "Three Pillars of Our Approach" section. It says one thing, well, in three sections. Restraint.

## What would have killed the brand (and was avoided)

- Generic "blue and white" wellness palette
- Stock photography of women smiling at the camera holding a water bottle
- "Holistic, integrative, evidence-based" adjective stack
- A trust badges row ("Featured in Vogue, Goop, Mindbodygreen")
- A three-column "Pillars: Mind, Body, Spirit" grid
- Centered hero with three-feature cards below

If you find yourself reaching for any of those, you have lost the seed. Go back.
